Precision and accuracy are both terms used to describe the quality of measurements, but they refer to different aspects: 1. Precision: Precision refers to how closely multiple measurements of the same quantity agree with each other. If measurements are precise, they cluster closely around the same value. Even if that value is far from the true value, as long as the measurements are consistent with each other, they are considered precise. 2. Accuracy: Accuracy refers to how close a measurement is to the true or target value. An accurate measurement is one that is very close to the actual value being measured. Accuracy takes into account both systematic errors (consistent inaccuracies) and random errors (inconsistent variations) in measurements. In summary, precision is about consistency among measurements, while accuracy focuses on the closeness of measurements to the true value.
